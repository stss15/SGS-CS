———————————————————

Obj 1: Distinguish between public and private data.
Obj 2: Explain how data becomes visible online through actions and settings.

———————————————————

Big Question:
“Which piece of information about you would cause the most damage if it became public?”

Task:
Write down three personal data items ranked from least to most sensitive.

———————————————————

6. CORE KEYWORDS (5 Keywords)

———————————————————

KW1: Public Data — information anyone can access.
KW2: Private Data — information intended only for you or trusted people.
KW3: Exposure — when data becomes visible unintentionally.
KW4: Metadata — hidden information automatically attached to content (e.g., time, location).
KW5: Privacy Settings — tools that control who sees your data.

Key Misconception

“If I only share with friends, my data can’t spread.”
Data can become public through screenshots, resharing, hacks, or privacy setting changes.

9. TRUE OR FALSE PLENARY (3 Questions)

———————————————————

Q1: “If I only share with friends, my data is safe.”
A1: False
Reason: Data can be shared, copied, or screenshot by others.

Q2: “Metadata isn’t real data.”
A2: False
Reason: Metadata can reveal time, place, and device information.

Q3: “Privacy settings guarantee complete safety.”
A3: False
Reason: Settings reduce risk but cannot prevent all exposure.
