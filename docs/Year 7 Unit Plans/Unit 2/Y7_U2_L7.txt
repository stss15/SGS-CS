———————————————————

Concept Name: Adaptation
Concept Icon: fa-arrows-rotate

Skill Icon: fa-shield-halved

6. CORE KEYWORDS (5 Keywords)

———————————————————

KW1: Cyberbullying — repeated harmful behaviour carried out through digital devices.
KW2: Harmful Contact — unwanted or unsafe messages or interactions from others.
KW3: Anonymity — hiding real identity online, which can change behaviour.
KW4: Escalation — when behaviour becomes more intense or unsafe over time.
KW5: Report & Block — digital tools used to stop harmful contact and alert trusted adults/platforms.

7. MINI TASK

———————————————————

Task Title: Safe or Unsafe?
Instruction:
Place each example into Safe Interaction or Unsafe Interaction based on behaviour and intention.

Col 1 Content
	•	A friend joking respectfully in a group chat
	•	Someone repeatedly messaging after being ignored
	•	A teacher commenting on a school assignment
	•	A stranger asking what school you go to
	•	A classmate posting a meme about you without permission

Col 2 Content
	•	Being added to a new group without being asked
	•	Receiving pressure to share personal photos
	•	Someone using multiple accounts to contact you
	•	A polite disagreement in a comment thread
	•	A message saying “Don’t tell anyone we’re talking”

Feedback (Correct Answers)

Safe Interaction:
	•	Friend joking respectfully (context matters)
	•	Teacher giving feedback
	•	Polite disagreement

Unsafe Interaction:
	•	Repeated messaging after being ignored
	•	Stranger asking what school you go to
	•	Posting a meme about you without permission
	•	Being added to a group without consent
	•	Pressure to share personal photos
	•	Multiple accounts contacting you
	•	“Don’t tell anyone we’re talking” (major red flag)

Key Misconception

“Cyberbullying is just normal drama.”
Drama ends quickly — cyberbullying is repeated, targeted, and harmful, and it always needs reporting.

9. TRUE OR FALSE PLENARY (3 Questions)

———————————————————

Q1: “If someone is joking, it can’t be cyberbullying.”
A1: False
Reason: If it hurts someone and continues, it’s bullying — regardless of intention.

Q2: “Blocking someone can make a situation worse.”
A2: False
Reason: Blocking removes access and protects you; platforms encourage it.

Q3: “Harmful contact only comes from strangers.”
A3: False
Reason: It often comes from peers, acquaintances, or friends.
