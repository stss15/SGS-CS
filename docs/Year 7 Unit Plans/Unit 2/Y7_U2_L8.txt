2. RETRIEVAL PRACTICE (5 Questions)

(From Lesson 7)

———————————————————

Q1: What is cyberbullying?
A1: Repeated harmful behaviour carried out through digital devices.

Q2: What is harmful contact?
A2: Unwanted or unsafe messages or interactions online.

Q3: What does anonymity mean online?
A3: Hiding or disguising your real identity.

Q4: True or False — “Harmful contact only comes from strangers.”
A4: False.

Q5: Give one example of unsafe behaviour online.
A5: Pressuring someone for personal photos / repeated messages after being ignored / “don’t tell anyone we’re talking”.

5. THE BIG QUESTION

———————————————————

Big Question:
“If someone saw only your online presence, would it reflect the person you want to be?”

Task:
Write down three words you want your online identity to communicate (e.g., creative, responsible, funny, supportive).

KW1: Digital Artefact — a digital creation that represents ideas or information.

7. MINI TASK

———————————————————

Task Title: Strong or Weak Identity?
Instruction:
Students sort examples into Strong Representation and Weak Representation based on clarity, safety, and how well they communicate a positive identity.

Col 1 Content
	•	A clear profile picture (no location clues)
	•	A chaotic slide layout with many fonts
	•	An example of a hobby or skill
	•	A username that includes your full name
	•	A short, positive quote

Col 2 Content
	•	A photo showing your house number
	•	Reposting random memes with no context
	•	A consistent colour scheme
	•	Using only emoji instead of text
	•	A screenshot showing notifications/messages

Feedback (Correct Answers)

Strong Representation:
	•	Clear profile picture (safe)
	•	Hobby or skill example
	•	Short positive quote
	•	Consistent colour scheme

Weak Representation:
	•	Full-name username
	•	Photo showing house number
	•	Chaotic layout
	•	Random memes with no meaning
	•	Using only emoji
	•	Screenshots showing private data

Key Misconception

“A positive digital identity must be perfect.”
It only needs to be authentic, safe, and intentional — not flawless or highly polished.

9. TRUE OR FALSE PLENARY (3 Questions)

———————————————————

Q1: “Design choices can affect how trustworthy your digital identity looks.”
A1: True
Reason: Layout and clarity influence audience perception.

Q2: “Using my full name in every username helps build a positive identity.”
A2: False
Reason: It increases risk and reduces privacy.

Q3: “A digital artefact should reflect the identity I want others to see, not everything about me.”
A3: True
Reason: Online identity is selective and purposeful.
