Lesson Number: L3
Lesson Title: How Online Behaviour Adapts and Changes
Unit Subtitle: Unit 2: Becoming a Digital Citizen

———————————————————

2. RETRIEVAL PRACTICE (5 Questions)

(From Lesson 2)

———————————————————

Q1: What is an algorithm?
A1: A set of rules a platform uses to decide what you see.

Q2: What is a recommendation system?
A2: A system that suggests content based on your online behaviour.

Q3: True or False — “Platforms show everyone the same content.”
A3: False — feeds are personalised.

Q4: What is a filter bubble?
A4: When you mainly see content similar to what you’ve previously engaged with.

Q5: Give one example of passive data collected by platforms.
A5: Watch time, scrolling behaviour, what you pause on.

3. CONCEPTS & SKILLS

———————————————————

Concept Name: Adaptation
Concept Icon: fa-arrows-rotate
Concept Definition: Changing behaviour in response to different online environments, expectations, and risks.

Skill Name: Digital Literacy
Skill Icon: fa-globe
Skill Definition: Understanding how to behave responsibly online and recognising when behaviour needs to adjust for safety, privacy, and wellbeing.

———————————————————

4. LESSON OBJECTIVES (3 Objectives)

———————————————————

Obj 1: Identify ways online behaviour changes across different platforms.
Obj 2: Explain how digital identity can evolve over time.
Obj 3: Recognise when behaviour needs to adapt for safety, privacy, or wellbeing.

———————————————————

5. THE BIG QUESTION

———————————————————

Big Question:
“Do you behave the same way on every app? Why or why not?”

Task:
Write down one app where you behave differently, how you behave there, and why.

———————————————————

6. CORE KEYWORDS (5 Keywords)

———————————————————

KW1: Adaptation — adjusting your behaviour for different digital spaces.
KW2: Online Norms — unwritten rules about how people behave on different platforms.
KW3: Digital Wellbeing — maintaining a healthy balance in online activity.
KW4: Online Reputation — how others view you based on your digital actions.
KW5: Context Collapse — when different audiences unexpectedly see the same content.

———————————————————

7. MINI TASK

———————————————————

Task Title: How Should You Adapt?
Instruction:
For each scenario, write one sentence describing how behaviour should change.

Col 1 Content
	•	Posting on a public social media account
	•	Messaging in a private group chat
	•	Writing a comment on a school platform

Col 2 Content
	•	Replying to a stranger online
	•	Sharing a photo that includes friends

Feedback (Correct Answers)

Feedback Col 1:
	•	Public → more cautious and thoughtful
	•	Private chat → relaxed tone, but still respectful
	•	School platform → formal, appropriate language

Feedback Col 2:
	•	Stranger → avoid sharing personal information
	•	Photo with friends → ask permission before posting

Key Misconception

“Online behaviour doesn’t affect real-life reputation.”
Students must understand that online actions contribute to long-term digital identity.

9. TRUE OR FALSE PLENARY (3 Questions)

———————————————————

Q1: “My online behaviour is always the same everywhere.”
A1: False
Reason: Different platforms have different expectations and norms.

Q2: “Adapting behaviour online means pretending to be someone else.”
A2: False
Reason: Adaptation is about safety and context, not dishonesty.

Q3: “Digital wellbeing has nothing to do with time spent online.”
A3: False
Reason: Excessive use can affect wellbeing and balance.
