2. RETRIEVAL PRACTICE (5 Questions)

(From Lesson 4)

———————————————————

Q1: What is public data?
A1: Information anyone can access.

Q2: What is private data?
A2: Information meant only for you or trusted people.

Q3: What is metadata?
A3: Hidden information attached to content (time, location, device).

Q4: True or False — “Privacy settings guarantee total safety.”
A4: False.

Q5: Why can photos taken at home be risky to share?
A5: Background details and metadata can reveal personal information.

5. THE BIG QUESTION

———————————————————

Big Question:
“If someone judged you only by your online actions, what would they think of you?”

Task:
Write down three actions you’ve taken online in the past month (likes, comments, searches, posts).
Then write: “What might someone assume from this?”

6. CORE KEYWORDS (5 Keywords)

———————————————————

KW1: Online Identity — how you present yourself online through actions and content.
KW2: Online Reputation — how others judge you based on your digital behaviour.
KW3: Permanence — the idea that online content can remain accessible even when deleted.
KW4: Digital Trace — small bits of data that contribute to your overall identity.
KW5: Self-Presentation — the deliberate choices you make about what to show or hide.

7. MINI TASK

———————————————————

Task Title: Identity Builder
Instruction:
Sort the items into Positive Reputation and Negative Reputation based on how they might be perceived by others (even if you didn’t intend it that way).

Col 1 Content
	•	Posting helpful answers
	•	Rude comments under a video
	•	Sharing a funny meme
	•	Using a consistent username
	•	Tagging friends without asking

Col 2 Content
	•	Sharing work you’re proud of
	•	Arguments in public comment sections
	•	Private jokes taken out of context
	•	Leaving supportive feedback on posts
	•	Posting at 2am regularly

Feedback (Correct Answers)

Feedback Col 1 — Likely Positive:
	•	Posting helpful answers
	•	Sharing a funny meme (context matters)
	•	Using a consistent username
	•	Leaving supportive feedback
	•	Sharing work you’re proud of

Feedback Col 2 — Likely Negative:
	•	Rude comments
	•	Arguments in public
	•	Tagging friends without permission
	•	Private jokes misunderstood out of context
	•	Posting at unusual hours (can signal stress or poor wellbeing)

Key Misconception

“Online reputation only matters when you’re older.”
Reputation begins forming immediately — classmates, teachers, and future peers see your digital actions.

9. TRUE OR FALSE PLENARY (3 Questions)

———————————————————

Q1: “A single post can change how people see my entire online identity.”
A1: True
Reason: First impressions online are often based on limited information.

Q2: “My online reputation is separate from my real-life reputation.”
A2: False
Reason: People often link both together.

Q3: “Only posting positive content is enough to protect my online identity.”
A3: False
Reason: Behaviour (comments, messages, reactions) is just as important as posts.
