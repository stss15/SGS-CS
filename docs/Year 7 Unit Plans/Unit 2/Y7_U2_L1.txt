3. CONCEPTS & SKILLS

———————————————————

Concept Name: Data
Concept Icon: fa-database
Concept Definition: Information created, shared, or left behind when using digital platforms—such as posts, searches, messages, and hidden metadata.

Skill Name: Digital Literacy
Skill Icon: fa-globe
Skill Definition: Understanding how online spaces work, what information you share, and how to behave safely and responsibly.

———————————————————

4. LESSON OBJECTIVES (3 Objectives)

———————————————————

Obj 1: Define digital citizenship, digital identity, and digital footprint.
Obj 2: Explain how everyday actions create online data.
Obj 3: Identify examples of public and private information online.

———————————————————

5. THE BIG QUESTION

———————————————————

Big Question: “If someone only saw your online data, what would they assume about you?”
Task: Write a quick list of online spaces where data about you exists (e.g., profile pictures, comments, search history).

———————————————————

6. CORE KEYWORDS (5 Keywords)

———————————————————

KW1: Digital Citizen — someone who participates responsibly online.
KW2: Digital Identity — how you present yourself and behave online.
KW3: Digital Footprint — the trail of information created by your online activity.
KW4: Personal Data — information that directly identifies you.
KW5: Public Data — information visible to anyone online.

7. MINI TASK

———————————————————

Task Title: Public or Private?
Instruction: Sort the items into Public or Private in your exercise book.

Col 1 Content
	•	Full name
	•	School email
	•	Username in a game
	•	Home address
	•	A photo you appear in

Col 2 Content
	•	Search history
	•	Your comment under a video
	•	Location data
	•	Phone number
	•	A “liked” post

Feedback (Correct Answers)

Public Data:
	•	Username in a game (often visible)
	•	A “liked” post
	•	Your comment under a video
	•	Sometimes: photos (depending on where they’re posted)

Private Data:
	•	Full name
	•	School email
	•	Home address
	•	Search history
	•	Location data
	•	Phone number
	•	Photo taken privately (context-dependent)

Key Misconception

“Deleting something removes it completely from the internet.”
Students must understand that deleted content may still exist elsewhere.

9. TRUE OR FALSE PLENARY (3 Questions)

———————————————————

Q1: “Private mode makes you anonymous.”
A1: False
Reason: Websites and networks can still track activity.

Q2: “My digital footprint doesn’t matter until I’m older.”
A2: False
Reason: Online information contributes to reputation at any age.

Q3: “A deleted post is always gone forever.”
A3: False
Reason: Copies and cached versions can remain even after deletion.
