Answer:

Lossy permanently deletes data. Lossless shrinks without losing data.

Deep Dive: Serial vs Parallel

Serial vs Parallel: The Trade-off

Which is better? It depends on the situation!

Serial

Pros:

  • Cheap (one wire)
  • Reliable over long distances
  • No skew (timing issues)

Cons: Slower (historically)

Use: USB, Internet, modern connections

Modern serial is actually faster than old parallel!

Parallel

Pros: Fast (historically)

Cons:

  • Expensive (thick cables with many wires)
  • Interference (crosstalk between wires)
  • Skew (bits arrive at different times)

Use: Internal CPU buses (short distances only)

Like a highway - fast but complex and expensive.

Deep Dive: Why USB Won

Why USB Won

What made USB the winner over all other connection types?

1. User Friendly

Impossible to plug in wrong (mostly).

Drivers load automatically.

No more "which way does this go?" frustration!

2. Power

It powers the device (5V).

No need for extra power bricks.

One cable does everything - data AND power!

3. Compatibility

Backwards compatible (old devices work with new ports).

Industry standard (everyone uses it).

Like a universal language - everyone speaks USB!

Question 1

"Parallel transmission is always faster than serial."

Answer: False

Modern serial (USB-C, Fibre) is faster than old parallel because of synchronization issues (skew).

Question 2

"A walkie-talkie is an example of Full-Duplex."

Answer: False

It is Half-Duplex (Only one person can speak at a time).

Question 3

"USB stands for Universal Serial Bus."

Answer: True

It is the industry standard for serial communication.
