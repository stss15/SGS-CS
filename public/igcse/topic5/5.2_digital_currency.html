The Old Way

Money was gold bars in a vault.

You could touch it.

The New Way

Money is just numbers on a screen.

Key Idea: Most money today has no physical form. It is Fiat Currency - just data.

Fiat vs Digital Currency

The Control Question

Who controls the money?

Analogy: Is it like a bank (one person in charge) or like a democracy (everyone votes)?

Digital Currency

Centralized

Controlled by Banks/Governments.

Examples: Apple Pay, PayPal, Credit Cards.

Cryptocurrency

Decentralized

Controlled by the Community/Algorithm.

Examples: Bitcoin, Ethereum.

Deep Dive: The Difference

Crypto Transaction Process

Centralized vs Decentralized

Centralized

The Path:

You Bank Central Bank Recipient

Pros: Regulated, stable, insured.

Cons: Not anonymous, relies on trust in the bank.

Decentralized

The Path:

You Recipient

(Peer-to-Peer, Wallet to Wallet)

Pros: Anonymous, secure, no middleman.

Cons: Volatile, no help if you lose your key.

The Security System

How do we trust a system with no bank in charge?

Key Feature: Once data is added, it cannot be changed (Immutable).

Like writing in permanent ink on a public noticeboard.

Deep Dive: How it Works

The Blockchain Block

The Unbreakable Chain

The Chain of Trust

1. The Block

Contains:

  • Data (Transaction details)
  • Timestamp (When it happened)
  • Hash (Digital Fingerprint - SHA-256)
  • Previous Hash (Link to last block)

Hash = Unique ID. Change anything, hash changes completely.

2. The Chain

Block 1 (Genesis) Block 2 Block 3

Each block contains the hash of the previous block.

Security: If you hack Block 2, its hash changes.

Block 3 sees the mismatch and the chain breaks.

3. The Network

Every computer (Node) has a copy of the entire chain.

To hack it: You'd need to hack 51% of ALL computers at once.

Virtually impossible.

Analogy: Like trying to change a book that millions of people have a copy of.

Question 1

"Digital currency and cryptocurrency are exactly the same."

Answer: False

Crypto is decentralized; Digital currency is usually centralized (like PayPal).

Question 2

"Blockchain data is stored on a single central server."

Answer: False

It is distributed across all computers in the network.

Question 3

"If you change data in a block, the hash remains the same."

Answer: False

The hash changes completely, breaking the chain.
