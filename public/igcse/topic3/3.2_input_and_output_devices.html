The Big Question

The Senses

How does a computer see, hear, and speak?

It needs ways to interact with the world.

I/O

Input = Data IN

Output = Information OUT

Like our senses and actions.

Key Idea: Without I/O devices, a computer is just a box of silicon. They're the bridge to the real world.

Reading the World

How do computers "read" physical objects?

2D Scanners

Documents to Images

Uses OCR (Optical Character Recognition) to convert to text

Like taking a photo and extracting the words.

3D Scanners

Objects to 3D Models

Used for CAD and 3D Printing

Like creating a digital copy of a real object.

Barcode Scanners

Laser reads black/white lines

Supermarkets, Libraries

Every product has one!

QR Codes

2D Barcodes (much more data)

Read by phones. Holds URLs, text, data

Like a barcode but smarter.

Deep Dive: Barcode vs QR

Barcode vs QR Code

Why did we need QR codes when barcodes already existed?

Barcode (1D)

Holds ~30 digits

Read horizontally only

Need laser scanner

Like a single line of text.

QR Code (2D)

Holds ~7000 digits (200x more!)

Read horizontally AND vertically

Read by camera (any phone)

Built-in error correction

Like a whole page of text in a square!

Barcode vs QR Code

Capturing Reality

How do we turn light and sound into digital data?

Digital Cameras

Light hits sensor (CCD/CMOS)

Converted to digital by ADC

Like your eye, but it saves what it sees.

Microphones

Sound vibrates diaphragm

Converted to digital by ADC

Like your ear, but it records what it hears.

ADC (Analogue to Digital Converter) is essential for all real-world input.

The real world is analogue (continuous). Computers need digital (discrete).

Deep Dive: Touch Technologies

Touch Technologies

How does a screen know you're touching it?

Resistive

Pressure sensitive - two layers touch

Cheap, works with gloves/stylus

Like pressing two sheets together.

Capacitive

Electrical charge - current flows to finger

Multi-touch, durable, needs bare skin

Like static electricity - your finger conducts!

Infra-red

Grid of light beams - finger breaks the beam

Durable, works with anything

Like a laser security system - break the beam!

Deep Dive: Control vs Monitoring

Sensors Cheat Sheet

Control vs Monitoring

Does the system just watch, or does it act?

Monitoring

Just watching

e.g., Weather Station, Patient Monitor

No action taken by system

Like a security camera - just records.

Control

Watching AND Acting

e.g., AC Unit, Greenhouse

Feedback Loop: Input → Process → Output → Input

Like a thermostat - senses and responds!

Press Down to reveal answers

Question 1

"A QR code holds less data than a barcode."

Answer: False

QR codes hold thousands of times more data (2D vs 1D).

Question 2

"Capacitive touch screens work with gloves."

Answer: False (Usually)

They rely on the electrical conductivity of skin. Resistive screens work with gloves.

Question 3

"Sensors send digital data directly to the CPU."

Answer: False

Sensors are Analogue. They need an ADC to convert data first.

Question 4

"OLED screens need a backlight."

Answer: False

OLED pixels generate their own light. LCDs need a backlight.
