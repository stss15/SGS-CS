Deep Dive: Types of Robots

Independent vs Dependent

Independent (Autonomous)

No human intervention needed.

Examples: Mars Rover, Roomba, Autonomous Car

Replaces the human.

Like a self-driving car - you just get in and it drives.

Dependent

Human interface required.

Examples: Robotic Surgery Arm (Surgeon controls it), Bomb Disposal Bot

Supplements the human.

Like a remote-controlled drone - you still control it.

The Factory Floor

Why do we hardly see humans in car factories?

Tasks: Welding, Spray Painting, Lifting, Assembly

All done by robots with precision and speed.

Deep Dive: Evaluation

Industry Evaluation

Advantages

  • Consistency: Perfect welds every time (no human error).
  • Productivity: 24/7 operation (no breaks, no sleep).
  • Safety: Handles toxic fumes/heavy loads (humans stay safe).

Disadvantages

  • Unemployment: Replaces low-skilled jobs (social impact).
  • Deskilling: Humans forget how to do the manual work.
  • Cost: High initial investment (but pays off long-term).

Driverless Future

How does a car drive itself?

The Tech

LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging), Radar, Cameras

Like eyes that never blink.

The Scenario

A car approaching a red light sees it and stops automatically.

No human needed to press the brake.

Deep Dive: Pros & Cons

Autonomous Vehicles: The Trade-off

Pros

  • Safer: No human error/distraction (no texting while driving).
  • Efficient: Smoother traffic flow (robots communicate with each other).
  • Accessible: For elderly/disabled (independence).

Cons

  • Hacking risks: If hacked, entire fleet could be compromised.
  • Ethical dilemmas: The Trolley Problem (who to save in a crash?).
  • Legal liability: Who is to blame? The owner? The manufacturer? The programmer?

Deep Dive: Precision

Precision Agriculture

Weeding

Robots can zap individual weeds with lasers.

Benefit: Reduces chemical herbicide use (eco-friendly).

Like a sniper - precise, not a shotgun.

Harvesting

Robots use cameras to pick only ripe fruit.

Benefit: Less waste, higher yield.

Like a picky shopper - only the best ones.

Question 1

"A drone controlled by a remote is an autonomous robot."

Answer: False

It is dependent (human controlled). Autonomous means it flies itself.

Question 2

"Robots in industry increase the cost of products."

Answer: False

In the long run, they reduce costs (efficiency, no wages).

Question 3

"LiDAR is used by autonomous cars to build a 3D map."

Answer: True

It uses lasers to 'see' the world.

Question 4

"A 'Chatbot' is a physical robot."

Answer: False

It is software (AI), not a robot (Physical).
