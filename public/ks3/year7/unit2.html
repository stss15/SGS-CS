Unit 2: Becoming a Digital Citizen

Digital Safety, Responsibility & Ethics

Unit Overview

Your online world is part of your real world. In this unit, you’ll discover how your digital identity is created, what your online actions reveal about you, and how your choices can protect—or harm—your privacy and wellbeing. You’ll explore how platforms track data, how misinformation spreads, why digital footprints matter, and how to build a positive online presence.

Unit Objectives

  • Understand online security, privacy, and digital citizenship.
  • Recognise harmful digital behaviours and how online actions affect reputation and wellbeing.
  • Explore how digital footprints are created and maintained.
  • Undertake creative digital projects across multiple applications.
  • Reflect on personal online identity and responsible participation online.

Concepts

This unit focuses on three St George’s Computer Science concepts:

Innovation

How new technologies influence the way people communicate, behave online, and build digital identities.

In this unit: we explore how platforms shape the way we present ourselves and how digital tools create new forms of identity.

Adaptation

How digital identities change over time and how online behaviour responds to platform norms and digital footprints.

In this unit: we look at how your online presence evolves and how everyday actions leave lasting traces.

Data

How information about you—posts, likes, searches, and hidden metadata—is collected, stored, and interpreted.

In this unit: we examine how digital footprints are created and what your online trail tells others about you.
