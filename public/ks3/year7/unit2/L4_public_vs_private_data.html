Concepts & Skills

Concept: Data

Information that can be shared, exposed, interpreted, or misused online — including posts, images, comments, and hidden metadata.

Skill: Data Handling

Categorising data, judging risk, and understanding how information becomes public.

Lesson Objectives

  • Distinguish between public and private data.
  • Explain how data becomes visible online through actions and settings.
  • Recognise risks linked to oversharing and exposure.

The Big Question

“Which piece of information about you would cause the most damage if it became public?”

Task: Task: Write down three personal data items ranked from least to most sensitive.

Core Keywords

  • Public Data — information anyone can access.
  • Private Data — information intended only for you or trusted people.
  • Exposure — when data becomes visible unintentionally.
  • Metadata — hidden information automatically attached to content (e.g., time, location).
  • Privacy Settings — tools that control who sees your data.

Key Misconception: “If I only share with friends, my data can’t spread.”

Data can become public through screenshots, resharing, hacks, or privacy setting changes.
