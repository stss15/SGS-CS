Question 1

What is the difference between public and private data?

Answer:

Public = anyone can see; Private = restricted access.

Question 2

What is metadata?

Answer:

Hidden data attached to files (e.g., location, time).

Question 3

True or False: Privacy settings make you 100% safe.

Answer: False.

Nothing is 100% secure.

Question 4

Give one example of data that seems private but might not be.

Answer:

Usernames, photos (background info), Wi-Fi names.

Question 5

What does “exposure” mean online?

Answer:

When data becomes visible unintentionally.

Concepts & Skills

Concept: Data

Understanding how information is shared, exposed, and interpreted online.

"How does Data become public online, even when you didn’t intend to share it?"

Skill: Data Handling

Being able to categorise data, judge risk, and understand exposure.

"How can Data Handling help you judge which information should stay private?"

Lesson Objectives

  • Distinguish clearly between public and private data.
  • Explain how data becomes visible online through actions and settings.
  • Recognise common risks linked to oversharing.

The Big Question

"If you knew someone was watching everything you did online, would you change your behaviour?"

Task: Write Yes/No and one thing you would stop doing.

Core Keywords

  • Surveillance — monitoring of behaviour and activities.
  • Tracking — following a user's journey across the internet.
  • Third Party — a company other than the one you are directly using.
  • Data Broker — a company that buys and sells personal information.
  • Encryption — scrambling data so only authorised people can read it.

Question 1

"Only hackers try to track what I do online."

Answer: False

Advertisers, companies, and governments also track you.

Question 2

"Free apps don't cost money because they sell my data."

Answer: True

If the product is free, YOU are the product.

Question 3

"Incognito mode makes me invisible to everyone."

Answer: False

It only stops your browser from saving history; websites and ISPs can still see you.
