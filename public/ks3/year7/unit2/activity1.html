Activity 1: Who is John Doe?

Investigating Digital Footprints

MAIN TASK – The John Doe Investigation

You will explore a set of images from a fictional person called John Doe. All of these images were found publicly online. Your job is to work out what you can learn about him from the clues in the pictures.

1. What You Need to Find

Using the 15 dossier images, find at least 10 pieces of information about John.

Your list must include:

  • John’s name
  • John’s date of birth (day + month)
  • The city John lives in

After that, you can find any other clues you discover.

2. How to Record Your Findings

Create a table in your exercise book:

Image Number What information does this image reveal? Should this be public or private? Why?
(e.g. 1)

Fill in the table as you look at the images. You can choose any details you spot — big or small. Some examples might include hobbies, routine, places he visits, things he likes, family information, or anything else you can reasonably work out from what you see.

3. Look Carefully

  • Not every clue is obvious.
  • Some images may reveal more than one piece of information.
  • Think about how different details link together.
  • Ask yourself: “What does this tell me about John?” and “Would this be safe to share?”

4. Reflection (Write This Under Your Table)

A. What picture can you build of John Doe?

Write 4–5 bullet points describing what kind of person John appears to be based only on the information you found.

B. What should have stayed private?

Choose three items from your table that you think should not have been shared publicly. For each one, write one sentence explaining the risk or concern.

5. Partner Discussion

Discuss your findings with the person next to you. Share one thing you discovered that you didn’t expect.

By the End of This Task, You Should Be Able To…

  • Explain the difference between public and private information online
  • Recognise how small pieces of data can build a picture of someone
  • Understand why sharing too much online can be risky
  • Describe what responsible digital behaviour looks like
