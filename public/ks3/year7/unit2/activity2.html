Activity 2: Data Detective

Uncovering App Permissions & Tracking

MAIN TASK – The Smartphone Investigation

You have been given access to a simulated smartphone running "MiniPhone OS". Your job is to investigate the apps installed on it and find out exactly what data they are trying to collect about you.

1. The Investigation Log

Before you start, draw this table in your exercise book. You will fill it in as you explore each app.

App Name What data did it ask for? Why does it want it? Impact of saying NO
Browser
Connectify
MapMe
SkyWatch

2. Your Missions

Follow the instructions in the "Messages" app on the phone. Here is a summary of what to look for:

  • Browser: Look at the Cookie Banner. What is the difference between "Necessary" and "All" cookies?
  • Connectify: Read the permissions carefully. Does a social app really need your location all the time? Check the Terms & Conditions for the "Data Retention" rule.
  • MapMe: Compare "While Using App" vs "Always Allow". Why would an app want to track you when you aren't using it?
  • SkyWatch: Try "Approximate Location". Do you still get the weather? What is the privacy benefit?

3. Reflection (Write This Under Your Table)

A. The "Greediest" App

Which app asked for the most data? Did it seem justified?

B. The Trade-off

Give one example where you had to choose between Privacy (keeping data safe) and Convenience (making the app easier to use). What did you choose and why?

4. Partner Discussion

Compare your "Data Footprint" score with your partner. Who has more items in their footprint? Why?

By the End of This Task, You Should Be Able To…

  • Identify common permissions apps ask for (Location, Photos, Tracking)
  • Explain the trade-offs between privacy and functionality
  • Understand that "Free" apps often pay for themselves with your data
  • Make informed choices about app permissions
