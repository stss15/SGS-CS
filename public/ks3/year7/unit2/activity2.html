Activity 2: Data Detective

Investigating Your Digital Trail

MAIN TASK – The Smartphone Investigation

You will explore a simulated smartphone running "MiniPhone OS". Your job is to investigate the apps installed on it and discover exactly what data they are trying to collect about you.

1. Your Mission

Open the Data Detective phone simulator and complete the following investigations:

  • Browser Cookies: Find out what information cookies store about you
  • Connectify T&Cs: Read the terms and conditions of the social media app
  • MapMe Location: Discover what the maps app knows about your whereabouts
  • FreeGame Investigation: Uncover what permissions the "free" game wants

2. How to Record Your Findings

For each app investigation, record in your exercise book:

App Name What data does it collect? Why might this be a concern?
(e.g. Browser)

3. Key Questions to Answer

  • What types of permissions do apps commonly ask for?
  • Why do "free" apps need so much access to your data?
  • What is the trade-off between convenience and privacy?
  • How do terms and conditions protect companies, not users?

4. Reflection (Write This Under Your Table)

A. The Trade-off Question

Would you still use an app if you knew it was tracking your location 24/7? Explain your reasoning.

B. What Would You Change?

If you could change one thing about how apps collect data, what would it be and why?

5. Class Discussion

After completing the investigation, be ready to discuss: "Why do we agree to terms and conditions we don't read?"

By the End of This Task, You Should Be Able To…

  • Identify common permissions apps ask for (Location, Photos, Tracking)
  • Explain the trade-offs between privacy and functionality
  • Understand that "Free" apps often pay for themselves with your data
  • Recognise why reading terms and conditions matters
