Concepts & Skills

Concept: Data

Understanding how information is shared, exposed, and interpreted online.

"How does Data become public online, even when you didn’t intend to share it?"

Skill: Data Handling

Being able to categorise data, judge risk, and understand exposure.

"How can Data Handling help you judge which information should stay private?"

Lesson Objectives

  • Distinguish clearly between public and private data.
  • Explain how data becomes visible online through actions and settings.
  • Recognise common risks linked to oversharing.

The Big Question

"Is it worth giving up your privacy to use free apps like TikTok or Instagram?"

Task: Write "Worth it" or "Not worth it" and one reason why.

Core Keywords

  • Exchange — trading one thing for another (e.g., data for access).
  • Business Model — how a company makes money.
  • Targeted Advertising — ads chosen specifically for you based on data.
  • Monetisation — turning something (like your attention) into money.
  • Terms of Service — the legal agreement you sign (often without reading).

Question 1

"If I don't pay money, the app has no value to the company."

Answer: False

Your data and attention are extremely valuable.

Question 2

"Companies only sell data if I say yes."

Answer: False

You usually say "yes" by agreeing to the Terms of Service without reading.

Question 3

"My attention is a product that is sold to advertisers."

Answer: True

This is the core of the "Attention Economy".
