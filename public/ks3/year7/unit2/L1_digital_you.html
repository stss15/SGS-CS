Concepts & Skills

Concept: Data

Information created, shared, or left behind when using digital platforms—such as posts, searches, messages, and hidden metadata.

Skill: Digital Literacy

Understanding how online spaces work, what information you share, and how to behave safely and responsibly.

Lesson Objectives

  • Define digital citizenship, digital identity, and digital footprint.
  • Explain how everyday actions create online data.
  • Identify examples of public and private information online.

The Big Question

“If someone only saw your online data, what would they assume about you?”

Task: Task: Write a quick list of online spaces where data about you exists (e.g., profile pictures, comments, search history).

Core Keywords

  • Digital Citizen — someone who participates responsibly online.
  • Digital Identity — how you present yourself and behave online.
  • Digital Footprint — the trail of information created by your online activity.
  • Personal Data — information that directly identifies you.
  • Public Data — information visible to anyone online.

Public vs. Private Data

Public Data

  • Username in a game
  • A “liked” post
  • Your comment under a video

Private Data

  • Full name
  • School email
  • Home address
  • Search history
  • Location data

Consequences: Identity theft, stalking, reputation damage, and targeted scams.

Question 1

“Private mode makes you anonymous.”

Answer: False

Websites and networks can still track activity.

Question 2

“My digital footprint doesn’t matter until I’m older.”

Answer: False

Online information contributes to reputation at any age.

Question 3

“A deleted post is always gone forever.”

Answer: False
