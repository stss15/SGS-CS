Activity 3: Swipe Scenarios

Making Decisions About Online Behaviour

MAIN TASK – Online Behaviour Sorting

In this activity, you will look at 16 real-life online scenarios and decide how you would judge each person's behaviour. This isn't about right or wrong answers — it's about thinking carefully about what's acceptable online and whether it matters if people know who you are.

1. How It Works

Each card has two sides:

  • Front: The situation — what's happening and where
  • Back: The action — what the person actually did

You'll sort each card into one of four categories:

← Good & Use Your Name

This is good — no need to hide who you are.

→ Good but Stay Anonymous

This is fine — but better to use a nickname.

↑ It Depends…

This one is complicated — you decide.

↓ Not OK Online

This behaviour shouldn't happen.

2. Written Work

After sorting all 16 cards, you'll complete written reflections in your exercise book:

From "Not OK Online" pile

Choose one card and explain why this behaviour is never acceptable.

From "Good & Use Your Name" pile

Choose one card and explain why it's okay to use your real identity here.

From "Good but Stay Anonymous" pile

Choose one card and explain why staying anonymous makes sense here.

From "It Depends…" pile

Choose 3-4 cards and explain what makes these situations complicated.

3. Discussion Questions

  • Why do people behave differently when they're anonymous?
  • Is it ever okay to be rude online if no one knows who you are?
  • How do you decide what to share publicly vs. privately?
  • What's the difference between adapting your behaviour and being fake?

By the End of This Task, You Should Be Able To…

  • Identify ways online behaviour changes across different platforms
  • Explain how digital identity can evolve over time
  • Recognise when behaviour needs to adapt for safety, privacy, or wellbeing
  • Make reasoned judgements about online actions and their consequences
