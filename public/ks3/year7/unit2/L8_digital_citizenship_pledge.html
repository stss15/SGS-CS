Concepts & Skills

Concept: Innovation

Using digital tools creatively to present ideas, identity, and information in new formats.

"How does Innovation allow you to use digital tools creatively to present ideas?"

Skill: Creativity in Technology

Designing a clear, thoughtful digital artefact using layout, images, structure, and purposeful choices.

"How can Creativity in Technology help you design a clear, thoughtful digital artefact?"

Lesson Objectives

  • Plan the content for a short “Digital Me” digital artefact.
  • Select appropriate images, text, and examples that represent a positive online identity.
  • Organise information clearly using good layout and design principles.

The Big Question

"If someone saw only your online presence, would it reflect the person you want to be?"

Task: Write down three words you want your online identity to communicate.

Core Keywords

  • Digital Artefact — a digital creation that represents ideas or information.
  • Self-Presentation — how you choose to represent yourself online.
  • Audience — the people who will view your work or profile.
  • Purposeful Design — choosing layout, colour, and content intentionally.
  • Positive Identity — presenting yourself in a way that is safe, respectful, and reflects who you want to be.

Question 1

"Design choices can affect how trustworthy your digital identity looks."

Answer: True

Layout and clarity influence audience perception.

Question 2

"Using my full name in every username helps build a positive identity."

Answer: False

It increases risk and reduces privacy.

Question 3

"A digital artefact should reflect the identity I want others to see, not everything about me."

Answer: True

Online identity is selective and purposeful.
