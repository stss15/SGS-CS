Concepts & Skills

Concept: Innovation

New technologies and features that change how we communicate, interact, and present ourselves online.

Skill: Data Handling

Understanding how platforms collect, interpret, and use the data created through your online behaviour.

Lesson Objectives

  • Describe how platforms collect data based on interactions.
  • Explain how recommendation systems influence what content you see.
  • Identify examples of online features created through technological innovation.

The Big Question

“Why do two people using the same app see completely different content?”

Task: Task: Write down two apps you use and what they show you first when you open them.

Core Keywords

  • Innovation — new technological developments that change user experience.
  • Algorithm — a set of rules a platform uses to decide what you see.
  • Recommendation System — suggests content based on your behaviour.
  • Filter Bubble — when a platform mainly shows content similar to what you already like.
  • Engagement — how you interact with content (likes, watch time, comments).

Feedback

Behaviours

  • Watching a video → watch time, completion rate, interests
  • Liking a post → preferences, engagement level
  • Commenting → tone, interests, interactions with others

More Behaviours

  • Scrolling past → what you ignore, what you avoid
  • Searching for something → direct interests and intent

Key Misconception: “Algorithms only use what I post.”

In reality, they use behaviour — watch time, pauses, scrolling, and searches matter more than posts.
