The trail of data you create while using the internet.

Full name, home address, school name, passwords, etc.

Concepts & Skills

Concept: Data

Information collected by apps and websites, often used to track behaviour and personalise content.

Skill: Digital Literacy

Understanding permissions, terms of service, and how to make informed choices about your privacy.

Lesson Objectives

  • Identify common permissions apps ask for (Location, Photos, Tracking).
  • Explain the trade-offs between privacy and functionality.
  • Understand that "Free" apps often pay for themselves with your data.

The Big Question

“Why do two people using the same app see completely different content?”

Task: Discussion: In pairs, discuss and write down: How does an app know what you like? List 3 specific 'signals' you give it.

Core Keywords

  • Cookies — small files websites save on your device to remember you.
  • Permissions — access rights you give to apps (e.g., Camera, Location).
  • Terms & Conditions — the legal rules you agree to when using a service.
  • Trade-off — giving up one thing (like privacy) to get another (like convenience).
  • Tracking — collecting data about your online behaviour across different sites/apps.

Plenary Discussion

Why do we agree to these things?

Benefits vs. Trade-offs

We often trade privacy for convenience.

The Trade-offs

Feature Benefit (Convenience) Trade-off (Privacy/Risk)
Cookies Remembers your login & preferences. Tracks your browsing history for ads.
Permissions Maps & filters work correctly. Apps know where you are & what you look like.
Terms & Conditions You get to use the app for free. You agree to their rules & data usage.

True or False?

Check your understanding:

  1. “Denying all cookies breaks every website.”
  2. “Apps only track you when you are using them.”
  3. “Terms and Conditions are legally binding contracts.”

Press Down to reveal answers

Question 1

“Denying all cookies breaks every website.”

Answer: False

It might make them less convenient, but they usually still work.

Question 2

“Apps only track you when you are using them.”

Answer: False

Many apps ask for "Always Allow" location or run in the background.

Question 3

“Terms and Conditions are legally binding contracts.”

Answer: True

When you click "I Agree", you are signing a contract.
