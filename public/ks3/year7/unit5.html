Unit 5: Digital Wellbeing & Smart Thinking

How Apps Hold Your Attention & How to Stay in Control

Unit Overview

This unit helps you understand how modern apps are designed to keep people watching, scrolling, or playing — and how to build healthy digital habits. You will look at real examples from the apps you use every day: notifications, endless scroll, autoplay, streaks, pop-ups, and recommended content. You will learn simple thinking tools (spotting patterns, simplifying ideas, breaking routines into steps) to make sense of how these designs work. The unit focuses on balance, attention, and wellbeing. You will create a short, clear “Digital Balance Guide” using easy, practical tips you can actually follow.

Unit Objectives

  • Recognise common app features that are designed to grab attention.
  • Explain how notifications, scrolling, colours, and sounds influence behaviour.
  • Spot patterns in their own tech habits and identify what helps or distracts them.
  • Break down one digital habit into steps to see where change is possible.
  • Understand why balance matters for focus, sleep, and wellbeing.
  • Produce a simple, helpful “Digital Balance Guide” that uses clear ideas and good judgement.

Concepts

This unit focuses on four St George’s Computer Science concepts:

Function

What something is designed to do.

In this unit: Students look at why apps use features like notifications, streaks, and autoplay.

Adaptation

Adjusting behaviour or habits.

In this unit: Students identify small changes they can make to stay in control of their tech use.

Form

How information is organised.

In this unit: Students simplify their wellbeing advice so it’s easy to understand and follow.

Logic

Rules behind behaviour.

In this unit: Students notice patterns in how apps behave and how users respond.
